AI Opportunity Assessment – BlueBush Customer Service Automation


---

Task 1: Identify an Opportunity for Task Automation (Approx. 350–400 words)

The current customer service process at BlueBush involves manually reviewing and responding to customer inquiries received via email, social media, and the company website. Each inquiry is individually assessed and categorised by staff before a response is drafted. Due to rapid customer growth and increasing inquiry volume, this manual triage process has resulted in an average response delay of approximately 48 hours. This delay is consistent with existing organisational procedures but is no longer sustainable at current growth levels. The reliance on manual handling also increases the risk of inconsistent responses and limits the team’s ability to scale efficiently.

The desired outcome of automation is to significantly reduce response times while maintaining a high level of customer satisfaction. Automating the initial triage and response process would allow the organisation to handle a larger volume of inquiries without increasing staffing requirements. This aligns with organisational productivity goals by reducing administrative workload, improving response consistency, and enabling staff to focus on more complex or high-value customer interactions. Maintaining accuracy is particularly important for BlueBush due to its environmentally focused branding, where incorrect or misleading information could damage customer trust.

Currently, the organisation uses standard communication tools such as email platforms, social media messaging systems, and website contact forms. There is no intelligent automation or AI-assisted response system in place. All responses are manually created based on staff knowledge and available documentation. These productivity goals and the opportunity for automation were confirmed through discussion with my supervisor (lecturer), who agreed that improving response efficiency while maintaining brand trust is a key organisational priority.

Task 2: Consider AI, ML, & DL Technologies (Approx. 350–450 words)

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is an artificial intelligence technique that combines information retrieval with text generation. Instead of relying solely on a trained model’s internal knowledge, RAG retrieves relevant information from a structured database before generating a response. The application procedure involves converting company FAQs and product documentation into vector embeddings, storing them in a searchable database, and retrieving relevant information when a user submits a query. This retrieved data is then used by the language model to generate a response grounded in factual information. This approach is highly suitable for BlueBush as it reduces the risk of incorrect or misleading responses, particularly in relation to environmental claims.

Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) is a machine learning technique used to efficiently fine-tune large language models. Rather than retraining an entire model, LoRA applies small adjustments to specific layers, allowing the system to adapt to a particular style or domain. The application procedure involves preparing a dataset of brand-specific customer interactions, training the model using LoRA to reflect the organisation’s tone and communication style, and integrating the fine-tuned model into the existing system. This technique is suitable as a secondary phase, as it improves consistency and brand alignment without requiring significant computational resources.

Deep learning-based conversational systems enable advanced voice interaction, including real-time communication and interruption handling. These systems typically integrate speech-to-text and text-to-speech technologies with a language model backend. The application procedure involves connecting voice processing systems to the AI response engine and deploying infrastructure capable of handling real-time interaction. While this approach offers a highly natural user experience, it requires significant computational resources and introduces additional risk if deployed before ensuring response accuracy. Therefore, it is more suitable as a future-stage enhancement rather than an initial implementation.

Task 3: Assess the Suitability of Each Technique (Approx. 350–400 words)

The three technologies identified can be implemented as part of a progressive system. Retrieval-Augmented Generation would form the foundation, providing accurate and reliable responses based on company data. Low-Rank Adaptation could then be introduced to refine the system’s communication style, ensuring alignment with the brand’s tone. Finally, deep learning voice systems could be integrated once the system has demonstrated consistent accuracy and reliability. This staged approach allows the organisation to minimise risk while gradually increasing system capability.

Each technology presents specific advantages and limitations. RAG offers high factual accuracy and significantly reduces the risk of incorrect responses, but it depends heavily on the quality and maintenance of the underlying database. LoRA is efficient and cost-effective, allowing targeted improvements to model behaviour, but it requires well-prepared training data to be effective. Deep learning voice systems provide a natural and engaging user experience, but they require substantial computational resources and introduce complexity in system design and deployment.

Several implementation risks were identified. These include the risk of AI-generated hallucinations leading to inaccurate environmental claims, potential inconsistencies if the knowledge base is not regularly updated, and increased infrastructure costs associated with advanced voice systems. These risks highlight the importance of prioritising accuracy before expanding functionality. These implementation risks were discussed with my supervisor (lecturer), who confirmed the importance of a staged deployment approach to minimise risk and ensure reliability.

Task 4: Recommendation and Justification (Approx. 450–500 words)

The recommended solution is the implementation of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system as the primary technology for automating customer inquiry handling. This approach directly addresses the organisation’s key challenge by reducing response times while ensuring that all generated responses are based on verified company information. This is particularly important for BlueBush, where maintaining trust through accurate environmental claims is critical.

RAG provides a strong foundation for automation because it balances efficiency with reliability. Unlike more complex solutions, it can be implemented with relatively low risk while delivering immediate improvements in productivity. By grounding responses in a controlled knowledge base, the system significantly reduces the likelihood of incorrect or misleading information being provided to customers.

The proposed implementation follows a progressive roadmap designed to support future expansion. The first phase involves deploying a RAG-based text response system to automate inquiry triage and provide accurate responses. Once this system is stable, a second phase can introduce LoRA fine-tuning to improve the tone and consistency of responses, aligning them more closely with the brand’s communication style. Additional phases may include multimodal features, such as image-based product support, followed by asynchronous voice interaction.

The final stage of the roadmap involves the potential integration of advanced deep learning voice systems capable of real-time, full-duplex communication. This would allow customers to interact with the system in a natural conversational manner. However, this stage is intentionally deferred until the system has demonstrated consistent accuracy and reliability. The overall system is designed with a modular architecture, ensuring that future technologies can be integrated without requiring a complete redesign.

This proposal was discussed with my supervisor (lecturer), who provided feedback emphasising the importance of starting with a reliable and low-risk solution. Based on this feedback, the recommendation was refined to prioritise RAG as the initial implementation while maintaining a clear pathway for future enhancements.
